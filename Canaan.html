Canaan Dog

Key Facts

  • Country of Origin: Israel
  • Date of Origin: Antiquity
  • First Use: Scavenger
  • Use Today: Livestock guarding, herding, tracking, companion
  • Life Expectancy: 12-13 years
  • Breed Size: Large
  • Weight Range: 35-55 lbs
The Canaan Dog was originally employed by the Bedouins as both a herder and guard dog in the Negev Desert. Today's breed, which was developed in the 1930s, has proved to be exceptionally versatile. During World War II, a number of Canaans were trained for mine detection; after the war, members of the breed were even used as guide dogs for the blind. Although rather aloof, it does make a good companion. Increasingly popular in its country of origin, the breed has now spread to the United States.

Breed Temperament Facts: Good with children. Good in warm climates. Good in cold climates. Needs minimal grooming. Good trainability. Good watchdog.
